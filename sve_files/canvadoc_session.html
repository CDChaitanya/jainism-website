Taxonomy of Jainism
One sense (touch): earth, water, fire, air bodies, nigodas (sub-atomical particles), plants
Two senses (add taste): worms, leeches, oysters, snails
Three senses (add smell): insects, spiders,
Four senses (add sight): butterflies, flies, bees
Five senses (add hearing): birds, reptiles, mammals, humans

Ācārāṅga Sūtra
The Ācārāṅga Sūtra, reflects a heterogeneous reality
when encouraging Jain ascetics who respectively use
three robes, two robes, and one robe to refrain from
begging for an additional robe (AS I.7.4 § 1; AS I.7.5 § 1;
AS I.7.6 § 1).
AS I.7.7 § 1: allows a naked Jain ascetic to use a loin cloth

Aparigraha - Parigraha
A Digambara argument in the Yuktiprabodha (17th century):
The wearing of clothes generates lice and their eggs, as well as
beings with three-sense faculties [touch, taste and smell].
Harm would come to those beings with each step, by the acts
of washing the clothes or placing them down somewhere.
Abhayadeva:
• Clothes: enhance the religious life and protect from cold.
• the Digambar naked muni, suffering from cold, might be
more inclined to light a fire
