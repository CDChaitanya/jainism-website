Samyak Jnana (Right Knowledge)

Definition:

Right Knowledge refers to a proper understanding of the true nature of reality, the universe, the soul, and the law of karma. It involves recognizing the distinctions between what is eternal (the soul) and what is temporary (material world), understanding the workings of karma, and realizing that spiritual development leads to liberation.

Story Example:

Nishi Shah, the guest lecturer, explained this point by giving a practical example. Suppose you want to become a billionaire. The first step is to acquire the right knowledge—knowing how businesses work, understanding the market dynamics, recognizing opportunities, and understanding how investments grow. Without this knowledge, you may make decisions based on ignorance, which could lead to failure.

Importance:

In Jainism, right knowledge is essential for spiritual progress. It clears away misconceptions and ignorance, allowing one to see the world and the soul as they truly are. Without right knowledge, a person cannot develop the right faith or practice the right conduct because they lack the understanding of what they are striving for.
