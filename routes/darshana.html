Samyak Darshana (Right Faith)

Definition:

Right Faith refers to having a correct understanding and belief in the Jain teachings, the path of non-violence (Ahimsa), and the possibility of liberation. It is the trust in the Jain scriptures, the guidance of the Tirthankaras (spiritual teachers), and the belief in the potential of the soul to achieve Moksha.

Story Example:

After acquiring the right knowledge, the next step is to have faith in your idea or business. For example, if you have identified a potential business opportunity, you need to believe in its success and trust in your vision. In the same way, in Jainism, right faith is about having trust in the spiritual path, believing that the practices outlined in Jainism will lead to liberation.

Importance:

Right Faith is essential because it forms the foundation for a person’s spiritual journey. Without faith in the teachings and practices of Jainism, even the right knowledge can lead to doubt or confusion. Faith sustains the individual, providing the conviction to follow the difficult path of self-discipline and inner purification.
