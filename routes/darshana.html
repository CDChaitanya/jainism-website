Samyak Darshana (Right Faith)

Definition:

Right Faith refers to having a correct understanding and belief in the Jain teachings, the path of non-violence (Ahimsa), and the possibility of liberation. It is the trust in the Jain scriptures, the guidance of the Tirthankaras (spiritual teachers), and the belief in the potential of the soul to achieve Moksha.

Story Example:

Continuing the example given by the guest lecture, in order to become a billionaire, after acquiring the right knowledge about how businesses work, the next step is to have the right faith in your idea. For instance, once you’ve learned about the market dynamics and identified a promising business opportunity, you need to believe in its potential and trust your vision. This faith in your idea fuels your perseverance and guides you through tough times, ensuring you stay committed to your goals, even when challenges arise.

Importance:

Right Faith is crucial because it provides the foundation for one's spiritual journey. Without faith in the teachings and practices of Jainism, even the right knowledge can lead to doubt and confusion. Faith gives the individual the strength and conviction needed to follow the challenging path of self-discipline and spiritual growth. It is through faith that one remains committed to the journey, trusting that each step brings them closer to spiritual liberation (moksha).
